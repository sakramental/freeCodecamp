All Kyokushin Tameshiwari Records: From the First One To the Current

Official tameshiwari in Kyokushin means breaking boards at tournaments.

Below are the most famous achievements of Kyokushin athletes in the tameshivari discipline

Andrey Kurtiy

There are four techniques in tameshiwari: Seiken (fore-fist), Sokuto (knife-foot), Enpi (elbow) and Shuto (knife-hand).

The standard board is 33 centimeters long, 21 centimeters wide, 24 millimeters thick and is made of soft wood (usually pine wood).

    Terutomo Yamazaki:

  • 24 boards (4 | 7 | 6 | 7), November 4, 1973, Tokyo, Japan The 5th All Japan Open Karate Tournament (2nd place at the tournament)
